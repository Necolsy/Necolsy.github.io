What I work on

Designing cities that breathe better.

My research combines wind-tunnel experiments, high-fidelity simulations, and machine learning to make the invisible behaviour of urban air measurable and actionable.

01

Urban wind & ventilation

Building porosity, street canyons, pollutant transport, and ventilation potential at the neighbourhood scale.

02

Validated computational fluid dynamics

RANS and LES workflows grounded in wind-tunnel evidence, with a focus on model fidelity and real design decisions.

03

Physics-informed AI

Surrogate modelling, graph neural networks, time-series learning, and LLM-assisted scientific workflows.

Research focus

From physical flow to useful intelligence.

A / 01

Porous urban form

How building openings and morphology reshape air exchange, cooling, and pollutant removal.

B / 02

Evidence-led CFD

Bringing wind-tunnel validation into simulations that can support urban and architectural practice.

C / 03

Learning with physics

Developing data-efficient models that respect both observed data and the underlying dynamics of the built environment.
